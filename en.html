Aboutthe Arcandina

María Elena Ordóñez and Pablo Palacios created the television series ARCANDINA to promote education in environmental and civic values. ARCANDINA was broadcast on television networks in several Latin American countries and the United States, reaching millions of children and their families with messages of environmental awareness. It has been recognized by institutions such as UNESCO, the National Wildlife Federation, and the Schwab Foundation of the World Economic Forum as one of the best environmental edu-communication strategies for children in the continent.

Meet the creators

MaríaElena Ordoñez

Educator, children's story writer, television scriptwriter and director, expert in communication and environmental education, with experience in developing audiovisual products and television programs for children and youth. In 2003, María Elena Ordóñez was named one of the 20 most outstanding Social Entrepreneurs worldwide by the Schwab Foundation of the World Economic Forum. In 2000, she was selected as a fellow by the Ashoka organization for social entrepreneurship and as a Social Leader by the Avina Foundation. She was recognized among the 40 exceptional women of Ecuador by Vistazo Magazine and among the 12 most outstanding Ecuadorians of 2002 by El Universo newspaper in Ecuador.

PabloPalacios Naranjo

Expert in multimedia communication and strategic communication planning, film director, photographer, and writer. He has served as a Communications Program Officer for Johns Hopkins University. Consultant and advisor for USAID, UNESCO, and UNICEF. President of the Arcandina Foundation and director of the Cimerron Cinema company in New York. Director and co-producer of the documentary series “Africa: the beat goes on,” about the contributions of Afro-descendants to the continent's culture and identity. His documentary Flags, Feathers and Lies was selected by the Library of Congress of the United States.

Contentmade for you

Rotary and Arcandina joined forces with the goal of promoting environmental education projects that raise awareness among new generations about the importance of water as a vital resource. Through a collaboration based on shared values, they have developed initiatives that include audiovisual materials focused on water conservation and ecosystem care. Together, we seek to foster positive change in how communities value and protect this indispensable resource for life.
